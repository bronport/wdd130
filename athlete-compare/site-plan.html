Athlete Compare — Site Plan

Author: Porter Bronson • Course: WDD130

Overview

Site Name

Athlete Compare (folder: athlete-compare)

Purpose

Athlete Compare helps fans quickly stack two professional athletes side-by-side using clear stats, awards, and context. The goal is to make comparisons fast, visual, and fair—so debates feel informed, not heated.

Audience

Sports fans, students, and casual analysts (teens to adults) who want simple, trustworthy, and visual comparisons across major sports (NBA, NFL, MLB, etc.). Mobile-first design for on-the-couch browsing, but great on desktop too.

Elevator Pitch (2–3 sentences)

Athlete Compare is a clean, interactive way to put two pros head-to-head. See the metrics that matter, from per-game stats and efficiency to accolades and longevity—then decide who truly comes out on top.

Navigation

Home Compare About / Methodology

The personal site will have a home page and two child pages: Compare and About/Methodology.

Content

Home (index.html)

  • Hero statement: “Compare athletes in seconds.” Short CTA button → Start Comparing.
  • How it works (3 steps): Choose sport → Pick two athletes → View visual head-to-head.
  • Sample comparison card (static): shows how results will look.
  • Notes on data: educational, fair-use text; no team logos; use royalty-free player photos or silhouettes.

Planned image(s): abstract sports collage or silhouette; small stat icons.

Compare (compare.html)

  • Form UI: sport dropdown, two athlete fields (typeahead later), “Compare” button.
  • Comparison grid: per-game stats, efficiency (e.g., PER/TS% if basketball), accolades (MVP, All-Star), career length.
  • Simple charts (static for class): bars for key stats, badges for awards.
  • Accessibility notes: high contrast labels, table with captions for screen readers.

Planned image(s): simple SVG icons for stats/awards.

About / Methodology (about.html)

  • Explain metrics and why they matter; define terms.
  • Data ethics: avoid copyrighted team logos; use public/royalty-free photos or self-made charts.
  • Limitations: small samples, era differences; how to interpret results.
  • Contact link or short credit section.

Planned image(s): methodology graphic or icons (scales, stopwatch, chart).

Wireframes

Home Wireframe

Hero with headline/CTA, three “how it works” steps, sample comparison card, footer.

Compare Wireframe

Form across top, comparison grid below with stat rows and mini bar charts.

About/Methodology Wireframe

Two-column text blocks with icons; callouts for definitions; contact link in footer.

Technical Notes

  • Mobile-first CSS with grid/flex; readable line length; WCAG AA contrast.
  • Images: use personal graphics, silhouettes, or royalty-free stock; keep file sizes small.
  • No external team/league logos to avoid trademark issues.
